# WEF Global Risk Reports - Experiment Notes

## What I did
Analyzed all 16 WEF Global Risk Reports (2007-2026) to create animated rank charts showing how perceived global risks changed over two decades.

## Data extraction approach
- Used the 2017 report's "Evolving Risks Landscape 2007-2017" figure (Figure 2) as the primary source for historical data (2007-2017 top 5 by likelihood and impact)
- Extracted top 10 rankings from individual reports for 2018-2021
- For 2023-2026: extracted COMPLETE rankings of all 32-34 surveyed risks (not just top 10) from the detailed figures in each report (Figure E in 2023/2024, Figure G in 2025, Figure 10 in 2026)
- Used PyMuPDF to extract text from PDFs, but much of the ranking data is in figures/charts that don't extract cleanly as text, so I cross-referenced multiple pages and the text descriptions

## Methodology changes discovered

### Era 1 (2007-2021): Likelihood & Impact
- Reports asked: "How likely is this risk?" and "How impactful would this risk be?"
- Two separate ranked lists
- 2007-2017: Top 5 per dimension
- 2018-2021: Expanded to Top 10

### Era 2 (2022): Transitional
- Report shifted to asking about "severity" rather than separate likelihood/impact
- Only published a 10-year severity ranking — no short-term ranking at all
- This makes 2022 a gap year for short-term data

### Era 3 (2023-2026): 2-year and 10-year Severity
- Reports now ask: "How severe is this risk over 2 years?" and "over 10 years?"
- Both horizons rank all ~33 risks

### Risk list changes
- The set of risks surveyed changed many times. New risks added, old ones retired, names changed.
- I created a normalization mapping to track risks across name changes (e.g., "Cyber attacks" → "Cyberattacks" → "Cyber insecurity" → "Cyber espionage and warfare")
- Some mappings are judgment calls (e.g., "Livelihood crises" and "Cost-of-living crisis" are related but not identical)

## Key findings

### Short-term (Likelihood → 2-year Severity)
1. **Pre-2020**: Economic risks dominated (asset collapse, fiscal crises, unemployment)
2. **2015-2021**: Environmental risks took over (extreme weather at #1 for 4 straight years)
3. **2022-2026**: Societal/geopolitical risks surged (misinformation, societal polarization, geoeconomic confrontation)

### Long-term (Impact → 10-year Severity)
1. **2007-2013**: Economic risks (financial failure, fiscal crises) dominated
2. **2014-2017**: Transition to environmental + security (WMDs, water crises, climate)
3. **2018-2026**: Environmental risks dominate (climate, extreme weather, biodiversity)
4. **AI risks** entered the top 10 in 2024 at #6 — a new entrant to watch

### Materialization of long-term → short-term
- 60-80% of long-term risk predictions appear as short-term concerns within 5 years
- **Always materialize**: Extreme weather, Climate change, Cyberattacks
- **Never materialize**: Biodiversity loss (perpetually "over the horizon"), Weapons of mass destruction
- **COVID was prescient**: Infectious diseases was in the 2020 long-term top 10 at #10, then rocketed to short-term top 5 in 2021

## Technical approach
- Python with PyMuPDF for PDF extraction
- Matplotlib for static bump charts and animated GIFs
- Data stored in `data.py` with full normalization mappings
- Charts generated by `charts.py`
- Showboat walkthrough in `walkthrough.md`
